### Andrej Karpathy (karpathy on X) — formerly Director of AI @Tesla, founding team @OpenAI

Karpathy shared a detailed workflow for building personal LLM-powered knowledge bases.
Karpathy 分享了一套详细的工作流，用于构建个人 LLM 驱动的知识库。

He collects source documents into a raw/ directory, then uses an LLM to incrementally compile them into a markdown wiki with summaries, backlinks, and categorized articles.
他将源文档收集到 raw/ 目录，然后用 LLM 将其逐步"编译"成带有摘要、反向链接和分类文章的 markdown wiki。

He uses Obsidian as the IDE frontend for viewing and navigating the wiki, while the LLM maintains all wiki content automatically.
他使用 Obsidian 作为 IDE 前端来浏览和导航 wiki，而 LLM 自动维护所有 wiki 内容。

Once the wiki grows large enough (~100 articles, ~400K words), he runs complex Q&A queries against it, getting output as markdown files, slideshows, or matplotlib images.
一旦 wiki 足够大（约 100 篇文章，约 40 万字），他就可以对其执行复杂的 Q&A 查询，输出结果为 markdown 文件、幻灯片或 matplotlib 图表。

He also runs LLM "health checks" to find inconsistencies, impute missing data, and discover new article candidates.
他还运行 LLM "健康检查"，以发现不一致之处、补全缺失数据、挖掘新文章候选。

*"I think there is room here for an incredible new product instead of a hacky collection of scripts."*
他认为这个方向有潜力诞生一款出色的新产品，而不仅仅是一堆临时脚本的拼凑。

He also extrapolated that every frontier LLM query could spawn a whole team of LLMs to build an ephemeral wiki, lint it, loop, and write a full report — "Way beyond a .decode()."
他进一步推断，每个前沿 LLM 的查询都可能派生出一整个 LLM 团队，去构建一个临时 wiki、进行 lint、循环迭代并生成完整报告 — "远不止一个 .decode() 那么简单。"

### Aaron Levie (levie on X) — CEO @Box

Levie shared a hard-won lesson from building AI agents: you must be "brutally unsentimental" about your architecture.
Levie 分享了构建 AI agent 的一条惨痛教训：你必须对自己的架构"毫不留情地抛弃旧情"。

The rough loop he described: build scaffolding around the LLM → models improve and render scaffolding redundant → remove old tech to unlock new gains → new harder problems emerge → repeat.
他描述的粗略循环是：围绕 LLM 构建脚手架 → 模型改进，脚手架变得冗余 → 移除旧技术以解锁新收益 → 出现新的更难问题 → 重复。

For Box Agent specifically, multiple components of the agent harness had to be overhauled because early mitigations (like custom chunking or retrieval tricks) started *hurting* performance once models got better at reasoning and context handling.
具体到 Box Agent，由于模型在推理和上下文处理方面的提升，早期的一些缓解措施（如自定义 chunking 或检索技巧）反而开始*损害*性能，多个 agent 组件不得不进行大改。

*"The main lesson is always make sure you're taking advantage of the frontier capabilities and don't become nostalgic around the tech you've already built."*
"核心教训是：始终确保你在利用前沿能力，不要对自己已构建的技术恋恋不舍。"

### Ryo Lu (ryolu_ on X) — Design @Cursor AI

Ryo published a design philosophy essay for Cursor 3, centered on the concept of "glass vs. black box."
Ryo 发表了一篇关于 Cursor 3 的设计哲学文章，核心概念是"玻璃 vs. 黑盒"。

His argument: the terminal was a black box, and early AI kept that black box while making it more addictive — you type a wish, pull the lever, accept or reject the whole output, and "see less and think less as the model became better."
他的论点是：终端是黑盒，早期 AI 保留了这个黑盒并让它更令人上瘾 — 你输入愿望，拉动杠杆，接受或拒绝全部输出，随着模型变得更好，你"看得更少，思考得更少"。

Cursor's answer is "glass": agents are visible, diffs are there, plans are editable, state is clear — you can go as deep as you want or stay as high as you need.
Cursor 的答案是"玻璃"：agent 是可见的，diff 就在那里，计划是可编辑的，状态是清晰的 — 你可以深入到任何程度，也可以保持在你需要的高度。

*"As AI gets more powerful, glass gets more important. Not because you need to watch every move. But because the best work happens when you know you can."*
"随着 AI 越来越强大，'玻璃'变得越来越重要。不是因为你需要监视每一步，而是因为当你知道自己可以的时候，最好的工作才会发生。"

He also announced Cursor 3 launch: simpler interface, works across local and cloud projects, starts simple then unfolds more tools when needed.
他还宣布了 Cursor 3 的发布：界面更简洁，支持本地和云端项目，从简单开始，按需展开更多工具。

### Training Data — "How Autonomous Labs Will Transform Scientific Research: Ginkgo Bioworks' Jason Kelly"

Published: March 24, 2026
https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLOhHNjZItNnMm5tdW61JpnyxeYH5NDDx8

This episode features Jason Kelly, founder and CEO of Ginkgo Bioworks, on how autonomous labs and AI reasoning models will fundamentally transform how science is done.
本期节目采访了 Ginkgo Bioworks 创始人兼 CEO Jason Kelly，探讨自主实验室和 AI 推理模型将如何从根本上改变科学研究的方式。

**Core argument:** Every previous tech revolution (Internet, social media) was irrelevant to biotech — "just back office IT crap." AI is different: it will change the fundamentals of how science is done.
**核心论点：** 以往每一次技术革命（互联网、社交媒体）对生物技术都无关紧要 — "不过是后台 IT 垃圾"。AI 不同：它将改变科学研究的基本方式。

**Autonomous lab breakthrough with OpenAI:** Ginkgo ran a project where an AI reasoning model was given a robotic lab and tasked with optimizing cell-free protein synthesis.
**与 OpenAI 的自主实验室突破：** Ginkgo 开展了一个项目，让 AI 推理模型掌控机器人实验室，任务是优化无细胞蛋白质合成。

After 4 rounds (~30,000 experiments), they beat the state of the art; after 6 rounds, they beat it by 40%.
经过 4 轮（约 30,000 次实验），他们超越了最先进水平；经过 6 轮，超出了 40%。

Kelly's thesis: the AI didn't need to be smarter — it just needed to run experiments 24/7 and share data across 100 parallel hypothesis threads daily, something human labs structurally cannot do.
Kelly 的论点是：AI 不需要更聪明 — 它只需要 7×24 小时运行实验，并在 100 个并行假设线程之间每天共享数据，这是人类实验室在结构上无法做到的。

**Why autonomous labs beat human labs (two unfair advantages):**
**自主实验室为何胜过人类实验室（两大不公平优势）：**

1. Information sharing: 100 AI scientists can share raw experimental data daily across all hypotheses, vs. human labs exchanging distilled papers every 1–2 years.
1. 信息共享：100 个 AI 科学家可以每天在所有假设之间共享原始实验数据，而人类实验室每 1-2 年才交换一次精炼论文。

2. Cost structure: today, <5% of research spending goes to reagents (the actual science); the rest is overhead (people, lab space). Autonomous labs flip this ratio toward ~90% reagents — a 10x improvement in data per dollar.
2. 成本结构：如今，不到 5% 的研究支出用于试剂（实际科学）；其余都是管理费用（人员、实验室空间）。自主实验室将这一比例翻转为约 90% 试剂 — 每美元数据量提升 10 倍。

**The "autonomous lab vs. cloud lab" distinction:** The key insight is that this isn't just automation — it's *autonomous* (flexible, high-variability work), analogous to how Waymo is not just a subway (fixed automation) but fully autonomous driving.
**"自主实验室 vs. 云实验室"的区别：** 关键洞察是这不仅仅是自动化 — 而是*自主*的（灵活、高变异性工作），类比于 Waymo 不只是地铁（固定自动化），而是完全自动驾驶。

**Coding models as lab UX:** Jason described mandating Claude Code/Codex for all lab protocol submissions — scientists write in plain language, the model handles the code, and mistakes get added to the skills file so they never repeat.
**编程模型作为实验室 UX：** Jason 描述了强制要求所有实验室协议提交使用 Claude Code/Codex — 科学家用自然语言写作，模型处理代码，错误被添加到技能文件中以避免重复。

**Project Genesis (DOE):** Ginkgo sold 97 robots to the Department of Energy for the White House's AI-for-science initiative, targeting doubling the pace of scientific discovery.
**创世纪项目（DOE）：** Ginkgo 向美国能源部出售了 97 台机器人，用于白宫的 AI 科学倡议，目标是将科学发现的速度翻倍。

**Geopolitical urgency:** China now accounts for 40%+ of biotech drug discoveries acquired by big pharma (up from <5% three years ago) — driven by having more experimentalists at lower cost.
**地缘政治紧迫性：** 中国现在占大型制药公司收购的生物技术药物发现的 40%+（三年前不足 5%）— 驱动因素是拥有更多成本更低的实验人员。

**Forward-looking signal:** If autonomous labs + reasoning models crack the experimental cycle, Kelly believes it won't just improve drug discovery — it could democratize science itself, letting anyone run experiments for as little as $39 (Ginkgo's current Cloud Lab pricing).
**前瞻性信号：** 如果自主实验室 + 推理模型破解了实验循环，Kelly 认为这不仅会改善药物发现 — 还可能使科学民主化，让任何人都能以低至 39 美元的价格运行实验（Ginkgo 目前的 Cloud Lab 定价）。

*"All of the previous revolutions in tech — Internet, social media — have been totally meaningless to biotechnology. Not this. This is actually gonna change the fundamentals of how we do science."*
"以往所有的技术革命 — 互联网、社交媒体 — 对生物技术都毫无意义。这次不同。这将真正改变我们做科学的基本方式。"
