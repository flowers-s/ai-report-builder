Aaron Levie pushed back on the common narrative that AI will simply replace today's jobs, arguing the opposite: AI will cause most roles to grow in complexity, not shrink.

*"What gets missed with AI productivity gains is that by and large, most roles will continue to be as sophisticated as the tools allow."*
他写道："关于 AI 提升生产力这件事，大家忽略了一点：大多数职位的复杂程度会随着工具能力的提升而持续上升。"

His reasoning: as everyone gains access to the same powerful tools, the bar for what constitutes the job rises naturally.
他的逻辑是：当所有人都能使用同样强大的工具时，"完成工作"的标准自然会水涨船高。

有 AI 辅助的工程师会去解决比没有 AI 的非工程师更难的问题；法律助理的工作不再是简单审阅合同——那个基准线会消失。

*"Simply put, AI will naturally cause most roles to actually grow in complexity rather than reduce in complexity, because we can do far more with the tools."*
他总结道："简单来说，AI 会自然导致大多数职位变得更复杂而非更简单，因为工具让我们能做的事情远比以前多。"

Continual learning is the real goal — and it's already on the path we're walking.
持续学习才是真正的目标——而这正是我们当前所走道路的延伸。
