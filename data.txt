Aaron Levie, Box CEO

Levie posted two detailed takes on enterprise AI architecture, both sharp and grounded in Box's actual product work.
Levie 发布了两篇关于企业 AI 架构的深度见解，均基于 Box 的实际产品实践，观点犀利且接地气。

On the knowledge problem: even the most advanced AI models can't be useful in enterprises without a context layer, because every employee has different access permissions, different workflows, and different data — and you can't train a model to "keep secrets" per user.
关于知识问题：即使是最先进的 AI 模型，在企业中没有上下文层也无法有效运作，因为每位员工拥有不同的访问权限、工作流程和数据——而且无法训练模型按用户"保守秘密"。

*"This is why the context layer is going to always be the core part of the AI stack for applied use cases."*
*"这就是为什么上下文层将始终是 AI 技术栈中应用场景的核心部分。"*

On agent design evolution: a year ago, building document-processing agents required chunking + embedding + similarity search as workarounds for small context windows.
关于 agent 设计的演进：一年前，构建文档处理 agent 需要通过分块 + 嵌入 + 相似度搜索来绕过小上下文窗口的限制。

如今，更好的上下文精度、工具调用和推理能力让 agent 能够更像人类一样工作——搜索、阅读完整文档、在感觉有误时重新查询。

*"As model progress continues on the dimensions of context accuracy, tool calling, advanced reasoning, and coding, agents are going to become insanely powerful."*
*"随着模型在上下文精度、工具调用、高级推理和编码等维度持续进步，agent 将变得无比强大。"*

No Priors — AI for Atoms: How Periodic Labs is Revolutionizing Materials Engineering with Co-Founder Liam Fedus

This episode features Liam Fedus, co-creator of ChatGPT and former VP of Post-Training at OpenAI, who left to found Periodic Labs — an AI foundation lab focused on materials science and chemistry.
本期节目邀请了 Liam Fedus，ChatGPT 的联合创造者、OpenAI 前训练后处理副总裁，他离职后创立了 Periodic Labs——一个专注于材料科学和化学的 AI 基础实验室。

**核心论点 | Core Thesis**

While most AI investment flows into software and language, Fedus argues the next frontier is the physical world — atoms, molecules, materials.
当大多数 AI 投资涌入软件和语言领域时，Fedus 认为下一个前沿是物理世界——原子、分子、材料。

AI foundation models trained on physics and chemistry data could compress decades of materials discovery into years.
在物理和化学数据上训练的 AI 基础模型，有望将数十年的材料发现压缩至数年内完成。

**关键洞察 | Key Insights**

• Fedus traces a through-line from his physics undergrad (dark matter research) → Google Brain → OpenAI → now materials AI. The physicists-in-AI pattern is not coincidental — physical intuition and mathematical rigor transfer directly.
  Fedus 的职业轨迹从物理本科（暗物质研究）→ Google Brain → OpenAI → 如今的材料 AI，一脉相承。物理学家涌入 AI 领域并非偶然——物理直觉和数学严谨性可以直接迁移。

• The post-training era at OpenAI (which Fedus led) established that how you train a model *after* pretraining — via RLHF, instruction tuning, etc. — is as consequential as the pretraining itself. He now applies this philosophy to scientific domains.
  Fedus 主导的 OpenAI 训练后处理时代证明：预训练*之后*如何训练模型（通过 RLHF、指令微调等）与预训练本身同等重要。他现在将这一哲学应用于科学领域。

• Materials science is uniquely data-starved compared to language — but that's a moat, not a bloat. Building the right training data infrastructure is itself a defensible competitive advantage.
  与语言领域相比，材料科学的数据极度匮乏——但这是护城河，而非负担。构建正确的训练数据基础设施本身就是可防御的竞争优势。

• The podcast discusses how model architectures from LLMs may not directly transfer to atomic/molecular simulation — new model families may be needed for different physical scales.
  播客讨论了 LLM 的模型架构可能无法直接迁移至原子/分子模拟——不同的物理尺度可能需要全新的模型家族。

**最值得关注的信号 | Forward-Looking Signal**

The "AI for atoms" thesis is early but the talent is arriving: if someone with Fedus's pedigree (literally co-created ChatGPT) is going all-in on materials AI, the scientific computing wave may be closer than mainstream AI discourse suggests.
"原子 AI"命题尚处早期，但顶尖人才已开始入场：如果像 Fedus 这样背景的人（字面意义上的 ChatGPT 联合创造者）正在全力押注材料 AI，那么科学计算浪潮或许比主流 AI 讨论所暗示的更近。
